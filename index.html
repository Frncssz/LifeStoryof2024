My Journey

Quarter 1

Welcoming 2024 by the Sea

As the sun dipped toward the horizon, painting the sky with soft hues of pink, blue, and gold, my family and I stood by the shore, feeling the cool breeze brush against our faces. The gentle waves lapped against the rocks, creating a soothing rhythm that matched the excitement in our hearts. It was the last evening of 2023, and we chose to welcome the new year by the sea our place of peace and reflection. A ferry in the distance sailed slowly, carrying passengers who, like us, were looking forward to new beginnings. We laughed, shared stories, and took in the beauty of the vast ocean stretching endlessly before us. As the clock neared midnight, we made our wishes for 2024 hoping for health, happiness, and success. Standing together, watching the sea embrace the coming year, I felt a deep sense of gratitude. No matter what lay ahead, I knew that as long as we had each other, every new year would always be special.

Quarter 2

A Student’s Journey in the City

Walking through the busy streets, I felt a mix of excitement and determination. The towering buildings around me were a reminder of how far I had come as a student, chasing my dreams in the heart of the city. With my backpack slung across my shoulder and my cap shielding my eyes from the soft evening light, I took steady steps toward my next destination whether it was school, a café to study, or simply a place to clear my thoughts. The streets buzzed with life, cars passing by, and people rushing to their own destinations. But I walked at my own pace, taking in the towering structures that once seemed intimidating but now felt like a second home. I had come to this city to learn, to grow, and to prove to myself that I was capable of achieving my goals. As I moved forward, I knew that this journey was just beginning. There would be challenges, long nights of studying, and moments of doubt, but standing amidst the city's towering skyline, I felt a renewed sense of purpose. Someday, I would look back at this moment and realize it was all worth it.
